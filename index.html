THE BIG CHANGES
⚡

Hybrid Power — Bigger & Bolder

The engines still have a 1.6L V6 petrol core, but the electric motor is now three times as powerful. For the first time ever, electric and petrol contribute equally — a genuine 50/50 split. The cars are actually more powerful overall.

Power Unit
🪽

Wings That Move Themselves

The front and rear wings automatically adjust as the car drives. On straights, they flatten out to reduce drag and go faster. In corners, they tilt back up to grip the road. Think of it like a plane adjusting its flaps automatically.

Active Aero
🌱

Greener Fuel

All cars must use 100% Advanced Sustainable Fuel — made from things like captured carbon, municipal waste and plant matter. It doesn't hurt performance, but it's a big step towards F1's goal of being net-zero carbon by 2030.

Sustainability
🚗

Smaller, Lighter Cars

The cars are shorter, narrower and 30kg lighter. This makes them more nimble and easier to handle, especially on tight street circuits like Monaco. Drivers say they feel noticeably more responsive than last year's cars.

Chassis
WHAT TO WATCH FOR ON RACE DAY
🚀

Boost Button

Drivers can press a button to unleash extra electric power whenever they like — for attacking, defending, or just going faster. They can use it all at once or spread it across the lap.

⚔️

Overtake Mode

If a car gets within 1 second of the car ahead, it gains extra electric power for a whole lap. It's like a turbo boost for overtaking — replacing the old DRS system.

🔋

Battery Management

Drivers must think carefully about when to charge their battery and when to spend the power. When they lift off the throttle, the car recharges. It's a whole new strategic puzzle every lap!

NEW FACES ON THE GRID
🏎️

Two Brand New Teams & Engine Makers

Cadillac (yes, the American car brand!) join as the 11th team, bringing the grid to 22 cars. Audi have also joined as an engine manufacturer for the first time. Ford are back too, supplying Red Bull's engines. Meanwhile Honda have moved from Red Bull to exclusively power Aston Martin.

JARGON BUSTER — WORDS YOU'LL HEAR
Active Aero
Wings that automatically move to help the car go faster on straights and grip better in corners. No driver input needed!
DRS (old system)
The old "overtaking flap" on the rear wing. It's gone — replaced by the new Active Aero and Overtake Mode systems.
ERS / Battery
Energy Recovery System — captures energy when braking or lifting off the throttle and stores it to use as electric boost power.
ICE
Internal Combustion Engine — the petrol part. In 2026 it shares power equally with the electric motor for the first time.
Recharge Mode
When a driver lifts off the accelerator on straights to top up the battery. You might see it as a brief dip in speed.
Downforce
The invisible force that pushes the car into the road at speed — like a wing in reverse — so it can take corners faster without sliding.
